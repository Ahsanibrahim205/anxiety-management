How can you learn
to manage your anxiety

  • When you recognise the feelings of anxiety like those described under Negative Personal Wellness you should jot them down in a diary or journal of some kind.
  • Give these feelings a rating on a scale of 1 to 100 (1 low and 100 very high) in terms of discomfort. Note: frequently this is not possible at the time but you should try to do it as soon as possible afterwards.
    • Situation Anxiety Level 1 - 100

  • Later, reflect back on the situation and try to identify the triggers as accurately as possible: When? Who? Where? What?
    For example:
    1. When? Sunday at 16.30h
    2. Where? In the kitchen of my friend’s house.
    3. Who? my wife in front of my two friends,
    4. What? My wife complaining that she did not know I was going away on a business trip this coming weekend.

  • Identify the thoughts and images that were swirling around your head in that specific situation.
    1. How did I react to the comments of my wife at that time? Upset and irritated, I felt my stomach turning over, thought “here we go again” …Made a comment about “I told you last night!” and began sweating and gulped my drink down, not really enjoying it.
    2. Why? I felt embarrassed in front of our friends, and undermined.
    3. What images were in my mind then? I had already told her last night – I could see us together at the dinner table when I had mentioned it.
    4. What was I telling myself? “Why is she nagging me now right in front of our friends? I have to do this to earn money to pay the mortgage; we shouldn’t be discussing this in public”
    5. What was I thinking? “This is embarrassing and totally unnecessary. Why doesn’t she remember? What are my friends going to think about this”
  • You need to look at how you can react in a more positive and less anxious way.
    1. What are the other person(s) thinking?
    2. How are your actions affecting them?
    3. How could you have handled the situation better?
  • How else could you have responded differently?
    1. What other ways could you have perceived the situation? Is the situation really as terrible as I imagined? Her remarks were just a normal reaction of someone who thought it was a bit late notice to hear about such a trip.
    2. What has paying the mortgage really got to do with it? Our friends have known us for years and won’t have been bothered by the conversation as they know we both lead extremely hectic lives.
    3. What other interpretations are there for the events which took place? She was not being aggressive but more surprised. She had maybe planned something important herself which with me being away could not take place easily.
  • Another method to prevent undue anxiety is to mentally rehearse a situation beforehand. Think about the situation, explore how you will act and react to different events that may happen. Look at all the ‘What ifs’ and work out a plan of action to cover every alternative you can think of.

  • By then making a note of what happened, how you dealt with it and how successful that was, you can start to build your skills at adapting which will eventually build your confidence and reduce your feelings of anxiety.
